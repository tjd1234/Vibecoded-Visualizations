Finding the Max of 500 Random Numbers

What's Going On Here?

To the left are 500 lines of random length. Starting from the left and going to the right, a red dot at the top of a line means that line is the tallest line seen so far. The first line always has a red dot, and the right-most red dot is the tallest line.

The red dots are how many times line A is executed by this min-finding algorithm:

array = ... 500 random numbers ...
maxSoFar = -1
for i from 0 to 499:
    if array[i] > maxSoFar then
        maxSoFar = array[i]  // line A

What's striking is that even though there are 500 lines, the number of red dots is relatively small.
